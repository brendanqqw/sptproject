A quick glance at the topics you'll learn

What are earthquakes and how do they occur? Earthquakes are the result of the Earth's tectonic plates moving and interacting along their boundaries. This interaction generates stress in the Earth's crust, which can lead to a sudden release of energy in the form of seismic waves, causing the ground to shake. Earthquakes occur at plate boundaries, where plates can diverge, converge, or slide past each other, as well as in some intraplate regions.

Understanding earthquake magnitude and intensity. Understanding earthquake magnitude involves measuring the energy released at the earthquake's source, often using scales like Richter or moment magnitude. In contrast, earthquake intensity assesses the effects at specific locations, varying based on factors like distance from the epicenter, local geology, and building construction. Magnitude indicates the earthquake's strength, while intensity describes its impact on the ground and structures.

Recognizing earthquake-prone regions. The Philippines is situated in a seismically active region due to its location along the Pacific Ring of Fire, making it earthquake-prone. The archipelago is at the convergent boundary of the Philippine Sea Plate and the Eurasian Plate, with several fault systems, including the Philippine Fault and the Manila Trench, running through the country. This geological setting increases the risk of earthquakes, and preparedness, adherence to building codes, and early warning systems are crucial to mitigate the impact of seismic events in the Philippines.

What are typhoons and how do they form? Typhoons are powerful tropical cyclones that form over warm ocean waters. They develop when sea surface temperatures exceed 26°C (79°F), causing warm, moist air to rise and create low-pressure systems. As the air spirals inward, it intensifies, forming a typhoon.

Identifying typhoon-prone areas. Typhoon-prone areas are typically found in the western Pacific Ocean, especially in regions like the Philippines, Japan, and Southeast Asia, where warm ocean waters provide ideal conditions for typhoon formation. Coastal regions near these areas are at higher risk of typhoon impact. Preparedness and early warning systems are vital for minimizing typhoon-related disasters in these regions.
